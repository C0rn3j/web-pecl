PEAR meeting in Amsterdam

PEAR's meeting is now planed for the 9th of may 2003 at 7:30 pm, Amsterdam. Lukas Smith has proposed a list of subjects that can be approached :

  • Quality vs. quantity
  • Possible regulations to avoid some recent conflicts that took place on the ML
  • PEAR CS (Conventions for method's names)
  • PFC RFC
  • PEAR Installer
  • PEAR on windows
  • PEARweb (voting system, comments system, packages proposal)
  • Promoting PEAR
  • PHP5
In order not to over-estimate this meeting goals, we can already define the following prioritys :
  • improve the general feeling inside PEAR community
  • have a better idea of what PEAR should be, and the best way to achieve it
  • brainstorming on PEAR's futur

LiveUser, a rights management and authentications package

LiveUser provides the necessary tools to manage permissions and authentications in a flexible and simple way. Its container system makes it possible to have different authentications and permissions sources. The integration in an existant sytem is possible by creating a specific container, permitting to always dispose of the same interface, no matter what the used methods are.
Great work was completed this week, a major update is planned for Monday, marking out a significant step towards the stable release.

Common methods names

Once again, a discussion about inputs/outputs functions took place on PEAR, some suggestions have received a good reception this time. It's mainly about data recovery under a specific format, for example : "toHtml" or "outputHtml", corresponding respectively to recovery and data printing in HTML, the preference going rather towards "display" instead of "output" in a WEB/Browser server context.

Quickform and templates system

This week marks the beggining of work on integration of the several systems of templates within this form handler, which will make page-settings of thoose easier. Alexy Borzov have provided the base skeleton as well as the IT[X] engine management available within the HTML_Template_IT package, with a little advance on the functionnalities.
